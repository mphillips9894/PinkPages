In need of emergency help?

If you are in need of immediate medical attention dial 911 immediately.
The list below isn't a comprehensive list of emergency services. These are some services The Pink Pages believes are catered to the LGBT* community.

National Suicide Prevention Lifeline

800-273-TALK (8255)
Online Chat
The Lifeline provides 24/7, free and confidential support for people in distress, prevention and crisis resources for you or your loved ones, and best practices for professionals.

1-888-7YOU CAN (1-888-796-8226)
333 North Braddock Ave.
Pittsburgh PA 15208
Comprehensive crisis services for Allegheny County residents, regardless of age or ability to pay. Services designed to meet your needs and preferences include:

  • confidential telephone conseling
  • mobile crisis teams that travel anywhere in Allegheny County
  • walk-in assistance, no apppointment needed
  • overnight/crisis residential (up to 72 hours)

Pittsburgh Action Against Rape (PAAR)

1-866-END-RAPE (1-866-363-7273)
81 S. 19th Street
Pittsburgh PA 15203
PAAR is a group of lawyers, health professionals, and activists that believe victims of sexual assault should have expert assistance directly following the commission of the crime. PAAR is one of the oldest rape crisis centers in the U.S., and the only organization in Allegheny County solely devoted to the issue of sexual violence.

Crisis Text Line

Text "HOME" to 741741
Crisis Text Line is free, 24/7 support for those in crisis. Text the hotline from anywhere in the USA to text with a trained Crisis Counselor. Their first priority is helping people move from a hot moment to a cool calm, guiding you to create a plan to stay safe and healthy.
